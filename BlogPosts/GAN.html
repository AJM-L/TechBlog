Generative Adversarial Networks

  Generative Adversarial Networks or GANs are a structure of neural networks used to create realistic synthetic data. They work by pitting two networks against eachother in a game where one network, the generator produces a fake image which is fed to the discriminator who returns the probability that the image is real. It is the goal of the generator to trick the discriminator by producing more and more realistic images, and it is the goal of the discriminator to accurately tell fake images from real ones. The competition between these two models allows the generator to produce realistic looking data from a provided dataset and some random noise. GANs are the models behind many contemporary generative tools, and are incredible complex and interesting generative systems.
